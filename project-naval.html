Data acquisition chain for naval artillery

  • Length : 6 month
  • Client: Naval Group
  • Website: naval-group.com
  • Completed: 28 Aug 2015

In 2015 I was studying in Toulon and I met with naval group engineers who presented me their obsolescence problematic :

100 mm gun of destroyer are a crucial piece of their ships and a equipment failure can have disastrous consequences.

To tackle this problematic we analyse previous history of such problems and identified the root cause of defaillance: wear. We found that the wear was increased on moving parts due to the heavy impact of movement during recoil and battery reentry movements.

The resulting project was to monitor those movement in an antonomous embedded system and to carry a real time analysis to detect eventual evolution and deformation in the stress.

We choose to work with an ARM development platform and ST Microelectronics Microelectromechanical accelerometers. The programming was done in C. When fully completed the project was deployed in future ships and has been successfuly used to improve quality of ships and customer satisfaction.
